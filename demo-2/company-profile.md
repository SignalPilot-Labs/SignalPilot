# NALA — Company Profile

> *"Make Money Move."* — Building payments for **#TheNextBillion**

NALA is an African-founded fintech building cross-border payment infrastructure for emerging markets. It operates two complementary businesses: a **consumer remittance app** (diaspora → Africa/Asia) and **Rafiki**, a B2B payments-and-stablecoin-settlement API that powers payouts and collections for global businesses. The thesis: payments in Africa and other emerging markets are "still 1% built," and the rails — not just the app — are the opportunity.

*Profile compiled June 2026 from public sources (nala.com, NALA Help Center, press coverage). Figures are company-reported unless noted.*

## 2. The Story

NALA's origins trace to **2016**, when Benjamin Fernandes — a former Tanzanian TV presenter who had worked on mobile money for TV subscriptions in East Africa — began developing the idea while on a full scholarship at **Stanford Graduate School of Business**. He returned to Tanzania to launch the company.

The early path was turbulent:
- After ~2.5 years of trying, NALA was accepted into **Y Combinator in 2019**.
- Fernandes received a **cease-and-desist** from Tanzania's largest mobile money provider, and the central bank ordered the first product (a domestic mobile-money aggregator) shut down.
- His **co-founder quit one week before Y Combinator Demo Day.**

NALA pivoted from a domestic Tanzanian app to **international remittances** (diaspora sending money home), which became its growth engine. It later layered on **Rafiki**, the B2B infrastructure business, recognizing that the payout rails it built for itself were valuable to other companies.

### Why it exists (the problem)
- Africans lose roughly **$8 billion/year** in remittance fees (~7–8% of transfer value).
- Asia's remittance-receiving region loses **$12B+** despite receiving ~$300B/year.
- Legacy cross-border payments into emerging markets are slow, opaque, and expensive. NALA's pitch is **speed + transparency + price**, built on direct integrations rather than legacy correspondent banking.

## 4. Products & Services

### A) Consumer App — "NALA, Send Money Globally"
A mobile-first remittance and multi-currency app for the diaspora.

**Core capabilities**
- International money transfer & FX conversion
- **Multi-currency account / wallet** — holds KES, EUR, GBP, USD, RWF, UGX, TZS, and **USDC** (stablecoin)
- Receive and hold money across borders
- **Account name verification** before sending (prevents misdirected funds)
- Real-time transaction notifications and tracking
- **"NALA Friends"** — peer-to-peer money splitting (UK, US, EU)
- 24/7 customer support

**Value props (company-stated)**
- **98% of transfers arrive within ~10 minutes**; ~95% of mobile-money transfers credited instantly (typical 2–5 min)
- **No hidden fees**, real-time FX rate visibility
- Daily transaction limits up to **$5,000 / £5,000 / €5,000**
- Stablecoin-backed funds, institutional-grade security

### B) Rafiki — B2B Payments Infrastructure (rafiki.com)
Launched **March 2024**. A compliant, licensed API bridge between **stablecoins and real-world payout networks**.

**What it does**
- Lets global businesses accept stablecoins (**USDC, USDT, PYUSD**), convert to **50+ local currencies**, and settle to **wallets or bank accounts** across Africa, UK, EU, and US.
- Connects to payout rails like **M-PESA, East African banks, SEPA, and ACH** — no exchanges, no multi-hop delays.
- Network reach: **~249 banks and ~26 mobile money services across 16 countries** (reported).

**Use cases:** global payroll, platform/marketplace payouts, treasury management, merchant collections.

**Traction (reported):** infrastructure payments volume grew **$0 → $1B in 18 months**; Rafiki grew ~**30x** in 12 months; revenue up ~**10x** year-over-year; counts **MoneyGram** among partners. Reported gross margins: **~64% (consumer)**, **~80% (Rafiki/B2B)**.

**Noah partnership (2026):** NALA + UK infra provider **Noah** launched an instant **stablecoin settlement network** for Africa & Asia — letting global firms collect in USD and pay out local currencies within minutes, targeting an ~$850bn emerging-markets payments opportunity.

## 6. Pricing Model

NALA monetizes through a mix of **small/zero transfer fees + a modest FX margin**:

- **Transfer fees:** Many corridors are **fee-free** (especially mobile-money). Bank-account payouts may carry a **~$0.99** fee; some corridors carry nominal charges to cover partner costs. Reported fees range roughly **$0.99–$5.99** per transfer depending on corridor/method.
- **FX margin:** A markup on the mid-market rate. Estimates vary by source from **~0.5–1%** (NALA's claim, real-time rates) to **~1.5%** (third-party review). Still typically far cheaper than banks.
- **Promos:** Referral credits and first-transfer bonuses are commonly offered.

*Net positioning:* cheaper and faster than banks and many legacy remittance providers, especially for mobile-money corridors; FX margin is the main "hidden" cost vs. a pure-interbank service like Wise.

## 8. Regulatory & Licensing

NALA emphasizes being **one of the most-licensed fintechs at its stage** — reported **~17 regulatory approvals/licenses** globally. Recent additions:

- **Bank of Uganda (Dec 2025):** Payment Service Provider (PSP) **and** Payment System Operator (PSO) licenses
- **Central Bank of Nigeria (2026):** International Money Transfer Operator (**IMTO**) license
- Operates under UK/EU/US money-transfer authorizations for its send-from markets

Licensing is strategically central — it's what lets Rafiki act as a **compliant, licensed bridge** rather than relying on third parties, and underpins the planned neobank.

## 9. Strategy & Direction

1. **Two-sided flywheel:** the consumer app builds payout rails and FX flow; Rafiki monetizes those rails for businesses (higher margin, faster growth).
2. **Stablecoins as settlement layer:** USDC/USDT/PYUSD to pre-fund and instantly settle corridors, cutting reliance on slow correspondent banking (Noah + MUFG-backed credit line both support this).
3. **Build a next-gen neobank** for emerging-market users (multi-currency account is the foothold).
4. **Expand corridors** — beyond Africa into Asia (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Philippines) and deeper licensing across destination markets.
5. **Own compliance** — heavy investment in licenses as a moat.

## 10. Competitive Landscape

- **Consumer remittance:** Wise, WorldRemit/Zepz (Sendwave), Remitly, LemFi, Chipper Cash, Western Union/MoneyGram.
- **B2B / infra & stablecoin rails:** Conduit, Bridge (Stripe), Yellow Card, Cellulant, Onafriq (MFS Africa), plus partners-turned-channels like MoneyGram and Noah.

**NALA's edge:** African-built distribution + deep licensing + a vertically integrated stack (consumer demand → owned rails → B2B monetization → stablecoin settlement).
